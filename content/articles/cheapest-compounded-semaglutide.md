### 1. Gala — $129/month (Cheapest Overall)

Gala's $129/month semaglutide (on the annual plan) is the lowest ongoing price we've verified, with no membership fee and free coaching plus dietitian access included. Their tirzepatide at $179/month is also the cheapest we've seen. The catch: the lowest rate requires the annual commitment — month-to-month runs higher.

Four dollars more than Gala, but with month-to-month flexibility, same-day approval, free coaching, and — notably — **price matching**: if you find a lower legitimate price, they'll match it. This is our top overall pick when flexibility matters.

### 3. TrimRX — $149/month

Flat $149/month with $140 off your first month and a money-back guarantee. A solid mid-range option with strong intro pricing.

### 4. Ro — $149-249/month

A large, well-established platform. Entry pricing starts around $149/month, but costs typically climb toward $249/month as your dose titrates up — the most common complaint with dose-based pricing. Good brand trust; budget for the higher end by month 4-6.

### 5. Mochi Health — $178/month effective

Mochi advertises "$99/month semaglutide," but that's the medication only — the required membership is $79/month, making the real cost $178. To their credit, the $99 medication price is **flat across all doses** (0.25mg to 2.5mg weekly), so your price never climbs as you titrate. Predictable, just not as cheap as the headline.

### 6. SkinnyRx — $199/month

The most expensive of our recommended platforms, but the only one offering **injectable, tablet, or sublingual** semaglutide — worth the premium if you want to avoid needles.

### 7. Henry Meds — $199-297/month

A well-known name with flat-rate pricing commonly around $297/month for compounded semaglutide (prepay plans bring it lower, and some tiers start near $199), plus a $49-99 initial consult fee. Reliable, but you're paying $70-160/month more than the top of this list for the same molecule.

<h2 id="hidden-costs" style="border-left: 4px solid #d97706; padding-left: 16px; color: #1c1917;">The 3 Hidden Costs That Inflate "Cheap" Semaglutide</h2>

**1. Membership fees.** Some platforms split "medication" and "membership" so the advertised number looks smaller. Always ask: *what is my total monthly charge?* Mochi's $99 becomes $178. Henry Meds adds a $49-99 consult fee upfront.

**2. Dose-based pricing.** Semaglutide titrates up over 4-5 months (typically 0.25mg → 1mg-2.4mg weekly). Platforms that price per dose — like Ro — can cost $100/month more at maintenance dose than at the starter dose you were quoted. Flat-rate platforms (Gala, Oak, Mochi's med price) don't do this.

**3. Intro rates that reset.** "$99 first month" promos commonly reset to $199+ in month two. Judge every platform by its **month-6 price**, not its month-1 price.

<h2 id="legality" style="border-left: 4px solid #7c3aed; padding-left: 16px; color: #1c1917;">Is Compounded Semaglutide Legal in 2026?</h2>

Yes — with an important distinction. Mass-production **503B** compounding of semaglutide ended in mid-2025 under FDA enforcement. What remains legal is **503A compounding**: licensed, state-regulated pharmacies preparing patient-specific prescriptions after a real prescriber evaluation. Every platform on this list operates on that model.

What to verify before buying from *any* platform:

- A licensed prescriber reviews your health history (intake form + review, often a video visit)
- Medication ships from a **licensed US compounding pharmacy** (ask which one)
- The platform requires standard eligibility (BMI 30+, or 27+ with a weight-related condition)
- Avoid: "no prescription needed" sellers, overseas pharmacies, and research-chemical sites — these are the dangerous ones

<div style="background: #f5f5f4; border: 1px solid #e7e5e4; border-radius: 12px; padding: 24px; margin: 20px 0;">

**Is cheaper compounded semaglutide lower quality?**

Not inherently — price differences mostly reflect business models (membership fees, marketing spend, prepay discounts), not the medication. What matters is the pharmacy: it should be a licensed US 503A compounding pharmacy. Ask the platform which pharmacy they use; legitimate ones will tell you.

</div>

<div style="background: #f5f5f4; border: 1px solid #e7e5e4; border-radius: 12px; padding: 24px; margin: 20px 0;">

**Do these prices include the doctor visit and shipping?**

On most flat-rate platforms (Gala, Oak Loves You, TrimRX, SkinnyRx), yes — provider evaluation and shipping are bundled. Henry Meds charges a separate initial consult fee, and Mochi charges ongoing membership. Always confirm the all-in total before checkout.

</div>

<div style="background: #f5f5f4; border: 1px solid #e7e5e4; border-radius: 12px; padding: 24px; margin: 20px 0;">

**Is tirzepatide worth the extra ~$50/month over semaglutide?**

Tirzepatide produces more weight loss on average (~21% vs ~15% of body weight in trials). At Gala's pricing ($179 vs $129), many people consider the gap worth it. Semaglutide remains the budget pick and has the largest body of outcome-trial evidence.

</div>

<div style="background: #f5f5f4; border: 1px solid #e7e5e4; border-radius: 12px; padding: 24px; margin: 20px 0;">

**Can I switch platforms to chase a lower price?**

Yes — prescriptions aren't locked to a platform, and switching mid-treatment is common. You'll complete a new intake with the new provider. Tell them your current dose so you don't restart titration from zero. (Oak's price matching exists precisely because of switchers.)
